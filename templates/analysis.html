Explore the performance of different machine learning models used in our predictions.

Model Accuracy Comparison

Accuracy measures the percentage of correctly predicted instances. Among the models used, Histogram-based Gradient Boosting Regressor and XGBoost stood out with a remarkable accuracy of 97.29%. Random Forest and Voting Regressor followed closely at 94.59%, demonstrating consistent performance.

Models like Bagging Regressor and Linear SVR were less accurate, emphasizing the need for advanced ensembling or fine-tuning.

Mean Absolute Error (MAE)

Mean Absolute Error (MAE) represents the average magnitude of errors in predictions, providing a clearer perspective on a model's precision. Random Forest achieved the lowest MAE at 2.51, closely followed by XGBoost at 2.64, proving their robustness in fare prediction.

Models with higher MAE, such as Linear SVR and Bagging Regressor, indicated potential room for improvement in handling complex patterns.

Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE)

RMSE provides a more weighted analysis of prediction errors, penalizing large deviations more than MAE. Random Forest demonstrated a solid RMSE of 4.41, with XGBoost also performing well at 4.88.

Models like Linear Regression and Linear SVR, while offering quicker predictions, had higher RMSE values, indicating limitations with outlier predictions.
